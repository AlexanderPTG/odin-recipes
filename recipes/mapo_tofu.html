Mapo Tofu

Description

Step into the world of Sichuan cuisine with our traditional Mapo Tofu recipe. With the exotic flavors of chile bean sauce, black bean sauce, and chile oil, this dish combines the perfect balance of spice and savory. Tender tofu and seasoned ground pork unite in a delectable symphony of flavors, offering a true taste of Sichuan cooking.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil, or as needed
  • 1/2 onion, cut into strips
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3/4 pound ground pork, or more to taste
  • 1/2 egg
  • 6 tablespoons chile bean sauce (doubanjiang)
  • 1/4 cup black bean sauce in chile oil
  • 1 teaspoon chile oil, or to taste
  • 1 (12 ounce) package tofu, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 7 tablespoons water
  • Salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 green onions, diced

Directions

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a saucepan over medium-high heat. Add onion and garlic; cook and stir until browned, 5 to 7 minutes.
  2. Mix pork and egg together in a bowl. Stir mixture into the saucepan. Saut√© until starting to turn color, 3 to 5 minutes.
  3. Stir chile bean sauce, black bean sauce, and chile oil into the pork mixture. Cover, reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer until pork is browned, 3 to 5 minutes.
  4. Add tofu; simmer until flavors are well blended, about 3 minutes. Season with salt and pepper and top with green onions.

Cook's Notes: After the pork mixture is cooked, you can add more water and sauce depending on your preference.
