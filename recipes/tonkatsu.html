Tonkatsu - Asian-Style Pork Chop

Description

Tonkatsu, a beloved Asian-style pork chop dish, is a mouthwatering delicacy that features thinly sliced, boneless pork chops coated in a golden, crispy layer of panko breadcrumbs. The tender pork, enhanced with a hint of garlic, is pan-fried to perfection, resulting in a delightful balance of flavors and textures. A delightful addition to any meal, Tonkatsu is a classic favorite that's sure to satisfy your craving for a delicious, crispy treat.

Ingredients

  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon minced garlic
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 cup vegetable oil for frying
  • 8 thin cut boneless pork chops
  • 1 1/2 cups panko crumbs

Directions

  1. In a medium bowl, mix together the eggs, milk, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  2. Heat oil in a large heavy skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Place the panko crumbs in a shallow bowl.
  4. Rinse pork chops with water, then dip in the egg mixture. Coat with panko crumbs, dip in the egg mixture again, then coat with another layer of panko crumbs.
  5. Lay coated chops on a plate until the rest are finished. If you have time, let them set for about 10 minutes, and the coating will set very well. If you wish to freeze the chops, now is the time.
  6. When the oil is very hot, place pork chops into the pan, and fry for about 5 minutes on each side, until golden brown.
